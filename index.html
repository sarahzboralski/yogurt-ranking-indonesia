The verdict, three ways
No single winner — it depends entirely on what you're optimizing for.
Cheapest calorie-cut
Cimory Low Fat

Lowest calories (80) and fat (1 g), cheapest per gram, no added sugar. Trade-off: only starter cultures and a little modified starch. The Normal Cimory is a different tub — 14 g sugar — don't grab it by mistake.

Gut health & clean
Biokul

100% yogurt, nothing added, plus real probiotics (L. acidophilus + Bifidobacterium). The cleanest everyday label. Trade-off: highest calories (130) and full fat.

Best macros, price no object
Hao Greek Protein

16 g protein and 0 g sugar per 100 g, five live strains, protein isolate, no preservatives. A tier above on nutrition — but ~3× the price of Cimory.

Read the fine print

  • Cimory has two look-alike tubs. Low Fat = 80 cal / 1 g fat / 6 g sugar (0 added). Normal = 120 cal / 4 g fat / 14 g sugar (6 g added sucrose). Same shelf, very different diet math.
  • "Sugar" isn't all equal. Cimory Low Fat, Heavenly Blush, Biokul and both Hao tubs carry no added sugar — their sugar is natural milk lactose. Yummy and Cimory Normal list sugar as an ingredient.
  • Yummy's high protein is partly built. It adds milk protein concentrate, modified starch and bovine gelatin, so the 7 g isn't purely from straining.
  • Hao Plain's cultures aren't printed on the panel shown, so it's marked unknown for the gut ranking. Its 0 g sugar / 0 g carb points to a genuinely strained yogurt.
  • Heavenly Blush uses beef gelatin; Yummy uses bovine gelatin — worth knowing for halal/dietary reasons (both carry local certification marks).
